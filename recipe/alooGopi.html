Dry Aloo Gobi Recipe

This recipe provides a detailed guide to making delicious Aloo Gobi (Potato and Cauliflower Dry).

Yields: 2-3 servings
Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 25 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 1 small head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 medium tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2.5 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1.5 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon vegetable masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves

Instructions:

  1. The vegetables: Wash and cube the potatoes. Wash the cauliflower and cut it into florets.
  2. Sauté the potatoes: Heat 2-3 tablespoons of oil in a kadai (wok or deep frying pan) over medium heat. Add the cubed potatoes and sauté for a few minutes until they start to brown slightly. Remove the potatoes from the kadai and set aside.
  3. Sauté the cauliflower: In the same kadai, add the cauliflower florets and sauté for a few minutes until they are lightly browned. Remove the cauliflower and set aside.
  4. Sauté the aromatics: Add 1 tablespoon of oil to the kadai. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it turns translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add spices and tomatoes: Add the slit green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté for a minute until the raw aroma of ginger-garlic disappears. Add the chopped tomato and sauté until it becomes mushy, about 3-4 minutes.
  6. Add dry spices: Reduce the flame to low. Add turmeric powder, chili powder, coriander powder, vegetable masala, and salt. Mix well and sauté for a minute until the spices release their aroma. Be careful not to burn the spices.
  7. Combine vegetables: Add the sautéed potatoes and cauliflower to the kadai. Mix well to coat the vegetables with the spice mixture.
  8. Cook the Aloo Gobi: Cover the kadai and cook for 2-3 minutes on low to medium heat, stirring occasionally, allowing the flavors to meld.
  9. Add garam masala: Open the lid and add the garam masala. Mix well.
  10. Dry roast: Increase the heat to medium. Cook the Aloo Gobi uncovered for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent it from sticking or burning. This process helps to dry out any excess moisture and enhance the flavors.
  11. Garnish and serve: Add the chopped coriander leaves and mix well.
  12. Serve hot: Aloo Gobi is now ready to be served. It pairs well with roti, paratha, rice, or naan.

Tips and Variations:

  • You can adjust the amount of chili powder according to your spice preference.
  • For a richer flavor, you can add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) to the oil along with the cumin seeds.
  • You can also add other vegetables like peas or carrots to this dish.
  • If you prefer a gravy-based Aloo Gobi, you can add a little water after adding the spices and let it simmer until the desired consistency is reached.
  • Ensure that the vegetables are cooked through but not mushy.
  • Adjust salt according to taste.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Aloo Gobi!
