Spicy Kadai Chickpea (or Rajma/Peas) Recipe

This recipe provides a detailed guide to making a flavorful and aromatic Spicy Kadai Chickpea dish, perfect for a quick and satisfying meal.

Yields: 2-3 servings
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 1 small head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds (kaduk)
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon chili flakes
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2.5 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable masala
  • 1 tablespoon tomato sauce
  • 1 (15-ounce) can chickpeas (or rajma/peas), drained and rinsed
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped.

Preparations:

  • Drain and rinse the chickpeas (or rajma/peas).
  • Finely chop the onion and coriander leaves.

Instructions:

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of coconut oil in a kadai or kuzhi ole tawa over medium heat.
  2. Add the mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add the curry leaves and sauté for a few seconds.
  4. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Add the chili flakes and sauté for 1 minute.
  6. Reduce the heat to low. Add the turmeric powder, chili powder, and vegetable masala. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the raw aroma of the spices disappears.
  7. Add tomato sauce and stir/mix well.
  8. Stir well to coat the chickpeas with the spice mixture.
  9. Cover the kadai and simmer for 5-8 minutes on low to medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent burning. Remove the lid after the first few minutes.
  10. Add fresh lemon juice and mix well.
  11. Garnish with finely chopped coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot with rice, roti, naan, or as a side dish.

Tips and Variations:

  • Adjust the amount of chili powder and chili flakes according to your spice preference.
  • For a richer flavor, use homemade vegetable masala.
  • You can add other vegetables like bell peppers or spinach to this dish.
  • If you would like a softer texture to the chickpeas, simmer for a longer period of time.
  • Ensure to stir occasionally to prevent burning.
  • Adjust salt and lemon juice according to taste.

Enjoy your delicious Spicy Kadai Chickpea!
